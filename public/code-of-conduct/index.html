Code of Conduct

Last updated: 13 May 2026

The European Mining Summit is committed to providing a professional, safe and welcoming environment for everyone — attendees, speakers, sponsors, staff, contractors and venue personnel. This Code of Conduct applies to all event spaces, online channels and associated social activities.

Our standards

We expect everyone at EMS to:

  • Treat others with respect and professional courtesy.
  • Engage in good-faith discussion. Disagreement on technical or commercial matters is welcome; personal attacks are not.
  • Respect confidentiality where it has been requested by a speaker, panellist or fellow attendee.
  • Comply with venue rules and reasonable instructions from EMS staff.

Unacceptable behaviour

The following behaviour will not be tolerated:

  • Harassment, intimidation or discrimination based on race, ethnicity, national origin, gender, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, age, disability, religion, or any other protected characteristic.
  • Unwelcome sexual attention or advances.
  • Threats or incitement of violence.
  • Sustained disruption of talks, panels or networking sessions.
  • Recording or photographing individuals without their consent in contexts where this is reasonably expected to be private.
  • Misrepresenting your identity or affiliation to gain access or business advantage.
  • Carrying weapons or any item that could reasonably endanger others.

Reporting

If you experience or witness behaviour that violates this Code of Conduct, please tell us as soon as you can. You can:

  • Speak to any EMS staff member on site — they are identifiable by lanyard or badge.
  • Email info@miningsummit.eu at any time, before, during or after the event.

Reports are treated confidentially. We will only share details with the people necessary to respond to the report or as required by law.

How we respond

The Organizer takes all reports seriously. Depending on the nature and severity of the conduct, responses may include:

  • A private warning.
  • A formal warning.
  • Removal from a specific session, area or activity.
  • Revocation of event credentials without refund.
  • A ban from future editions of EMS.
  • Notification of law enforcement where the conduct involves a criminal offence.

Sponsors and speakers are held to the same standard as attendees, and may have their sponsorship or speaking slot withdrawn for serious or repeated breaches.

Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all EMS-related activity, including the main conference programme at Tanssin Talo, networking events, sponsor sessions, EMS communications channels, and any social gathering officially associated with EMS.

Contact

Questions about this Code of Conduct should be directed to info@miningsummit.eu.
